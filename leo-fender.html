Leo Fender

The Father of Modern Electric Guitars

    Leo Fender Facts:

  • Leo Fender's full name is Clarence Leonidas Fender.
  • Unknown to most people, Leo Fender had pursued a career in accounting before getting involved with musical instruments.
  • Leo Fender had no formal education-training in electronics. HIs experience was strictly self-taught.
  • Leo opened his "Fender Radio Service" shop in 1938. The shop was intended to service radios, but bands and musicians began approaching his shop in search of public address systems and amplifiers for lap steel guitars.
  • The Stratocaster, Leo's second electric guitar design was famously used by Buddy Holly, Chuck Berry, Jimi Hendrix and Eric Clapton.
  • Despite building guitars for a living, Leo Fender barely knew how to play one.
  • In 1968, Leo Fender had to sell "Fender Electric Instrument Manufacturing Company" to CBS due to an illness. He unexpectedly fully recovered and continued building instruments under the names "Music Man" and "G&L".

Leo Fender was an inventor and founder of the Fender Electric Instrument Manufacturing Company, a company that is now world-renowned for their extensive and distinguished electric guitars and bass guitars. Although Leo did not invent the electric guitar, Leo Fender's claim to fame was designing the first commercially successful electric guitar; the "Broadcaster" (later renamed the "Telecaster"). Leo's other popular instruments include the famed "Stratocaster" electric guitar and the "P-Bass" bass guitar.
